Reddit is a genuinely excellent business trapped in a governance structure designed to benefit insiders at the expense of public shareholders. The core platform is exceptional: a defensible content moat strengthening in the AI era, revenue accelerating at $2.2B (69% YoY), operating margins expanding from deep negative to +20%, and $691M in operating cash flow. All six forensic models return clean signals. The Piotroski F-Score improved from 5 to 8. Cash conversion quality is excellent at 1.30x.

But the investment case has critical fault lines. Governance is among the most entrenched in public tech — a triple-class structure with a Huffman-Advance voting agreement giving the CEO unchecked control with no sunset provision (entrenchment score: 7/10). Executive compensation is tied to Adjusted EBITDA that excludes their own $343M stock-based compensation, creating a circular incentive structure that was rewarded at 225% in FY2024 despite a GAAP loss of $484M.

The diversification narrative sold to IPO investors is underdelivering. Content licensing revenue grew from $15M to $115M to $140M, but growth collapsed from 653% to 22%, and footnotes reveal substantially all licensing revenue comes from just 2 partners. Meanwhile, advertising concentration is actually worsening (91% → 94%), not improving.

User growth is decelerating sharply — US DAUq growth has fallen from 59% to 7%, and 55% of daily active users are logged-out, lower-monetizing visitors arriving via Google search. This creates a double vulnerability: search dependency + monetization ceiling.

The near-zero effective tax rate ($286M SBC windfall + $172M NOL release) flatters current earnings by ~$0.80/share and will normalize over 2-3 years. The $1B buyback program, while signaling confidence, covers only 86% of annual SBC dilution — it's a dilution offset, not a capital return.

The biggest hidden optionality is international monetization. With ~50% of users but only 19% of revenue, closing the 4x ARPU gap represents a $400-600M opportunity. Data licensing to additional AI partners could add $160-360M. Total addressable upside is $800M-1.5B in incremental annual revenue over 3-5 years.

Bottom line: Exceptional business, concerning governance, decelerating growth engines. The stock's trajectory depends on whether monetization gains can outrun user growth deceleration — and whether public shareholders are comfortable being structurally voiceless passengers.
