Advanced Micro Devices, Inc. is a fabless semiconductor company that designs and sells CPUs, GPUs, FPGAs, and adaptive computing solutions. The company operates across three reportable segments: Data Center (48% of FY2025 revenue at $16.6B), Client and Gaming (42% at $14.6B), and Embedded (10% at $3.5B). AMD competes against NVIDIA in AI accelerators, Intel in server and client CPUs, and Broadcom and Qualcomm across adjacent markets. The company is 100% reliant on TSMC for advanced-node silicon manufacturing.

FY2025 was AMD's strongest fiscal year on record by most financial measures. Revenue grew 34.3% to $34.6B from $25.8B in FY2024, itself an improvement from $22.7B in FY2023 — representing a two-year CAGR of 23.6%. Gross profit reached $17.2B at a 49.5% GAAP margin, stable from FY2024's 49.4%. Operating income surged 94.4% to $3.7B, though $2.25B in acquisition-related intangible amortization (from the 2022 Xilinx acquisition) and $1.64B in stock-based compensation flow through as real expenses reducing GAAP operating margin to 10.7% — while management's preferred non-GAAP operating margin ran approximately 24-26%. GAAP net income more than doubled to $4.3B ($2.67 basic EPS), aided by a $103M tax benefit producing an effective tax rate of -2.5%.

The Central Forensic Finding: The most important discovery from this investigation is the persistent opacity surrounding AMD's AI GPU business. Data Center revenue grew 32.2% to $16.6B — but Data Center operating margin compressed 600 basis points from 27.7% to 21.7% despite that revenue growth. AMD's CFO Jean Hu acknowledged in Q2 2025 that "MI margins are a little bit below corporate average," yet management refused — across all four quarters of FY2025 — to disclose GPU-specific revenue or margin figures. This opacity was confirmed by seven independent investigation layers. Analysts including Stacy Rasgon (Bernstein), Timothy Arcuri (UBS), and Vivek Arya (Bank of America) probed every quarter and were systematically redirected to "segment-level guidance." The result: investors cannot independently assess whether AMD's fastest-growing product line generates attractive economics or is buying market share at negative-to-marginal operating contribution.

The forensic scorecard is reassuringly clean. Beneish M-Score for FY2025 vs FY2024 is -2.67, well below the -1.78 manipulation threshold (UNLIKELY_MANIPULATOR, high confidence). Altman Z-Score improved to 3.72 (SAFE zone). Piotroski F-Score is 7/9, strong across all four profitability criteria and both efficiency criteria, with only leverage scores failing (long-term debt increased $627M and 15M new shares were issued). Sloan Accrual Ratio is -0.034, indicating cash earnings comfortably exceed reported earnings. Benford's Law chi-square test passes (9.22 vs. critical 15.51). Cash conversion quality (OCF/NI) runs at approximately 1.6x, structurally elevated by $2.25B annual intangible amortization and $1.64B SBC.

Management guidance accuracy was exceptional throughout FY2025. Revenue beats accelerated quarter over quarter: +4.2%, +4.1%, +5.7%, and +7.7% above guidance midpoints in Q1-Q4, giving AMD a sandbagging score of 7.5/10. Non-GAAP gross margin guidance of 54% was delivered exactly in three of four quarters. The $1.5B China export control headwind was disclosed proactively in Q1 2025 and managed transparently through the year. MI350 sampling, MI355 competitive benchmarking, and ZT Systems acquisition execution all proceeded on or ahead of schedule. The OpenAI partnership for first gigawatt of MI450 GPU capacity validates AMD's competitive credibility at the highest level of AI infrastructure deployment.

However, the investigation reveals meaningful governance concerns embedded within this strong performance. Stock-based compensation grew from $379M in FY2021 to $1.64B in FY2025 — a 44% CAGR — and now represents 37.8% of GAAP net income and 44.3% of GAAP operating income. Management's annual bonuses are determined by non-GAAP metrics that explicitly exclude SBC, creating a structural incentive to issue more stock compensation without consequence to measured performance. The board approved adding 65 million more shares to the equity incentive plan (4% potential dilution). The TSR performance benchmark was downgraded from the S&P 500 Information Technology Index to the broader S&P 500 — lowering the bar for executive equity vesting exactly when semiconductor stocks were significantly outperforming the market. No insider open-market purchases were detected across 94 Form 4 filings spanning 19 months.

At $221.53 per share (April 8, 2026), AMD trades at 83.3x trailing GAAP P/E, 79.8x EV/EBITDA, and 10.2x EV/Revenue. Forward P/E using analyst consensus is 20.5x — implying approximately $17.6B in forward earnings, roughly 4x FY2025's $4.3B GAAP net income. VIS intrinsic value models range from $10.89/share (EPV) to $125.67/share (Graham Formula), with the central Graham Number at $48.01/share — all below current price. The VIS verdict is "Expensive." The central investor question for 2026 is whether AMD's GPU business generates sustainable profits at scale or whether the company is buying AI market share at the expense of per-share economics.
