Advanced Micro Devices has executed a remarkable transformation from a diversified semiconductor company to an AI-infrastructure growth engine. In FY2025, revenue surged 34% to $34.6B, operating income nearly doubled to $3.7B, and estimated free cash flow inflected to ~$5.7B (up from $1.7B). The Data Center segment — now 48% of revenue — grew on the back of MI300/MI325/MI350 AI GPU shipments and continued EPYC server CPU share gains. Two landmark warrant deals with OpenAI and Meta Platforms, each granting 160 million shares at $0.01 exercise price tied to GPU purchase commitments (up to 6 GW each), represent the strongest validation of AMD's competitive position against NVIDIA in the AI accelerator market. Clean forensic scores across all models (Beneish UNLIKELY_MANIPULATOR, Altman SAFE, Piotroski 7/9, Benford PASS, Sloan -0.034) confirm earnings quality.

However, our investigation reveals significant information asymmetry that investors must navigate. The single most important finding — confirmed by 7 of 13 independent analytical layers — is that GPU revenue and margin economics are systematically obscured behind segment-level reporting. Data Center operating margins compressed 600 basis points (from 27.7% to 21.7%) despite 32% revenue growth, suggesting AMD may be pricing GPUs aggressively to buy market share. SBC at $1.64B (44% of operating income) is excluded from the non-GAAP metrics that determine executive bonuses, creating a structural incentive misalignment. The segment merger of Client and Gaming hides what was a 58% Gaming revenue collapse in FY2024. And sovereign AI pipeline claims ('40+ engagements,' 'tens of billions') lack revenue attribution or timelines.

The central question for investors is whether AMD's GPU business generates sustainable profits at scale, or whether the company is buying market share at the expense of per-share economics. Management's 100% guidance accuracy on revenue and margins suggests deliberate sandbagging (model 3-5% above midpoint), but the refusal to break out GPU economics — the fastest-growing product line — means investors cannot independently assess the quality of AI-driven growth. The warrant deals with OpenAI and Meta create 320M shares of potential dilution (~20% of outstanding) offset by massive committed GPU purchases. AMD's trajectory depends on MI400/MI450 execution, ROCm ecosystem adoption, and whether Data Center margins stabilize as GPU mix matures.

★ Verdicts

Fundamentally Strong
▣ Business Quality

AMD is fundamentally sound with accelerating revenue growth (+34% to $34.6B), expanding margins (operating margin tripled from 1.8% to 10.7% over two years), and clean forensic scores (Beneish M-Score -2.67, Altman Z-Score 3.72, Piotroski 7/9). The Data Center segment grew at a 60% two-year CAGR, EPYC CPUs have gained server share for 33 consecutive quarters, and the Client/Gaming segment posted a strong recovery (+51%). FCF margin expanded from ~4.5% to ~16.6%. The balance sheet carries minimal debt ($3.2B against $63B equity) with $10.6B in liquid assets. The only fundamental concern is Data Center margin compression (-600bps despite 32% revenue growth), suggesting GPU economics may not scale as favorably as CPU economics historically did.

Trust With Caution
⚙ Management Quality

Management trust score of 72/100 from the L10 transcript synthesis reflects a split picture. On execution: Lisa Su's team delivered 4 consecutive revenue guidance beats with accelerating magnitude (+4.2% to +7.7% above midpoint), shipped MI350/MI355 ahead of schedule, and proactively disclosed a $1.5B China export control headwind. On transparency: management runs a deliberate information control strategy — GPU revenue is never broken out, GPU margins are acknowledged as 'below corporate average' but never quantified, SBC ($1.64B, 44% of operating income) is never discussed on calls, and 10-K disclosure specificity declined year-over-year. 94 insider Form 4 filings with zero insider buying, while SBC is excluded from bonus-determining metrics, creates structural misalignment. TSR benchmark was downgraded from S&P 500 IT to S&P 500.

Strong But Decelerating
⏰ Trajectory Quality

AMD is on an upward trajectory powered by AI infrastructure buildout, validated by landmark GPU warrant deals with OpenAI (Oct 2025) and Meta (Feb 2026) securing up to 12 GW of Instinct GPU commitments. The MI350/MI355 product ramp is ahead of schedule, MI450 is committed for OpenAI's first gigawatt deployment, and the Helios rack-scale platform signals system-level ambitions. However, Data Center growth decelerated from +94% to +32%, and quarterly sequential revenue growth slowed through H1 2025 (16.9% to 9.1% to 3.3%) before reaccelerating in Q3 (+20.3%). The NVIDIA competitive overhang remains — AMD's ROCm ecosystem lags CUDA, and NVIDIA's B300/Blackwell Ultra could erode AMD's inference advantage. Base case FY2026 revenue: $40-44B (+15-27%). Key risk: MI400/MI450 transition air pocket in H1 2026.
