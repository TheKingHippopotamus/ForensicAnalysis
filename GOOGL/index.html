Alphabet Inc. delivered a landmark FY2025, posting $402.8B in revenue (+15.1%), $132.2B in net income (+32.0%), and diluted EPS of $10.81 (+34.5%). Google Cloud was the standout, accelerating to 35.8% growth ($58.7B) and now representing 14.6% of total revenue — up from 10.8% just two years ago. Google Search grew 13.4% to $224.5B, defying fears of AI-driven disruption, while the Subscriptions/Platforms/Devices segment surged 19.1% to $48.0B. Gross margins expanded for the fourth consecutive year to 59.7%, and the balance sheet remains fortress-grade with a net cash position of $84.8B and minimal leverage (debt/EBITDA of 0.18x). The company's Altman Z-Score of 4.12 and Beneish M-Score of -2.41 confirm financial health with no manipulation signals.

Beneath the headline strength, however, several forensic findings warrant investor attention. Operating income growth decelerated sharply from 33.3% (FY2024) to 14.8% (FY2025), while net income growth of 32% was substantially inflated by $29.8B in non-operating income — representing 22.5% of net income from volatile, non-core sources. Operating expenses grew 22% versus revenue growth of 15%, reversing the cost discipline narrative that defined FY2024. Quarterly CapEx nearly doubled from ~$13B/quarter in 2024 to ~$24B/quarter in H2 2025, and management has signaled $175-185B for FY2026 — the largest annual capital expenditure program in corporate history.

The most consequential risks are structural rather than financial. The dual-class share structure allows founders with ~3.86% economic ownership to maintain majority voting control, authorizing this unprecedented capital commitment without meaningful shareholder recourse. The DOJ antitrust case — targeting Search default agreements worth $20B+ annually — is a top-3 risk in the 10-K yet received zero discussion across four quarterly earnings calls. And 750 million Gemini monthly active users generating zero advertising revenue represents the largest unquantified transition risk in technology. Management's 91% promise-keeping record and conservative guidance bias provide comfort, but the increasing gap between what the 10-K discloses and what earnings calls discuss is a forensic concern that has intensified throughout FY2025.

★ Verdicts

BUSINESS Confidence: HIGH
Strong — Firing on Nearly All Cylinders

Alphabet's core business is firing on nearly all cylinders. Revenue accelerated for the second consecutive year to $402.8B (+15.1%), powered by Google Cloud's 35.8% surge to $58.7B and Search's resilient 13.4% growth to $224.5B. Gross margins expanded to 59.7% — the fourth straight year of improvement. The Subscriptions/Platforms/Devices line crossed $48B at 19% growth, reducing advertising dependency to 73% of revenue (from 77% two years ago). However, operating margin plateaued at 32% as AI infrastructure costs caught up with efficiency gains, and Google Network's structural decline continued. The business engine is strong but increasingly capital-intensive.

MANAGEMENT Confidence: HIGH
Credible but Curated — Trust Score 62/100

Management credibility is high on backward-looking execution — a 91% promise-keeping rate on verifiable claims and a consistent sandbagging pattern that benefits investors on the upside. However, forward-looking transparency is deteriorating. The largest capex commitment in corporate history ($175-185B FY2026E) comes with zero disclosed ROI metrics. Paid click and CPC metrics were quietly dropped in Q4. The DOJ antitrust case — a top-3 risk in the 10-K — received zero discussion across four earnings calls. Management's escalating AI triumphalism inversely correlates with their willingness to address cannibalization dynamics, cost discipline erosion, and the silent 40% buyback reduction. Trust score: 62/100 — credible but curated.

TRAJECTORY Confidence: MEDIUM-HIGH
Cautiously Bullish with Material Blind Spots

Alphabet is navigating the most consequential capital allocation decision in its history. The bull case is compelling: Cloud backlog at $240B with Apple as a new anchor tenant, Search proving resilient against AI alternatives, and full-stack vertical integration (models + TPUs + cloud + distribution) creating a genuine moat. The bear case centers on three unquantified risks: (1) $180B annual capex with no disclosed ROI framework, (2) 750M Gemini users generating zero ad revenue while potentially cannibalizing Search, and (3) DOJ antitrust remedies that could force changes to $294.7B in advertising defaults. Near-term trajectory favors continued acceleration, but the 12-month outlook hinges on whether AI investment translates to revenue faster than depreciation compresses margins. Cautiously bullish with material blind spots.
